Pre-Survey
Estimate your current partisan score on a scale between -5 to 5.

The stronger the negative value, the more left-leaning. Example news sources: Huffington Post, New York Times, Mother Jones
The stronger the positive value, the more right-leaning. Example news sources: Fox News, Breitbart, National Review
Values closer to zero are associated with centrist perspectives. Example news sources: The Economist, Wall Street Journal, BBC News
Partisan Alignment
What do the partisan scores tell us?
Based on the news media you consume, having a negative score indicates consuming more 'left wing' content. Conversely, a positive score is indicative of 'right wing' content, and scores close to zero indicate 'centrist' content.

What does content reliability tell us?
High = Factual news coverage, attention to detail
Mixed = Factual content quality varies per author on the site
Low = Little attention to fact-checking and/or propagation of false news.
